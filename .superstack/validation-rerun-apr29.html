Option A — RECOMMENDED · Crypto-native B2B (agencies, DAOs, contractors)

ICP: Web3 agencies, DAO treasuries paying contractors, crypto-native SaaS billing in stables, hackathon teams, dev shops. People who already hold USDC and want to invoice / get paid in it without spinning up custom infra.

Why this works: They live on X and Telegram (you can reach them today), the "should I use crypto" objection is pre-cleared, sales cycle is days not months, and your existing product (USDC invoices, payment links, Blinks, T+0) is exactly what they need.

What you cut: KYB-heavy onboarding (move it to Settings → Compliance), cannabis-specific copy, off-ramp to USD bank (DAO treasuries don't need it day 1).

First customer plausible in: 2–3 weeks. Post a thread on X showing a $500 invoice → settled in 2 seconds, ask "who wants the SDK?" Demo in DMs.

Risk: Crypto-native B2B is a lower-revenue-per-customer market. But it gets you to any revenue, which unlocks everything else.

Option B · Reachable high-risk verticals (CBD/hemp, firearms, adult)

ICP: CBD/hemp brands, FFL firearms dealers, adult content platforms — all non-cannabis "high-risk" categories that get strangled by Stripe and pay 5–10% in processor fees, but live online and respond to outbound.

Why this works: Same value prop ("we're the rail high-risk processors won't touch"), but the buyers have public emails, run paid ads, and are SMB-scale founders who answer LinkedIn. CBD/hemp in particular has 100k+ Shopify-class merchants.

Risk: You already have these as "industry pages" but de-prioritized them. Reactivating them = admitting cannabis-first was wrong, which is fine.

Option C · Persist on cannabis but completely change distribution

What it requires: (1) hire/partner with a cannabis-industry BD person who has operator relationships this month; (2) book a booth at MJBizCon Fall 2026; (3) sponsor 1 cannabis newsletter (Cannabis Business Times, MJBizDaily); (4) find one paid pilot via warm intro and turn that into 5 referrals.

When to pick this: Only if you already know someone in cannabis and just hadn't asked them yet. Otherwise: don't.
