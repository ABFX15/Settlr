Pricing — how you actually make money

The core pricing insight: peptide vendors mentally anchor on two different numbers, and you price against both. Their card processor costs 8–15% + reserves + chargebacks (your "look how cheap we are" story). Their raw-crypto cost is ~0% but the product is broken (your "look how much better we are" story). So: keep the headline % low to beat the crypto anchor, and earn real margin on the off-ramp + SaaS, where the comparison is to expensive cards. Don't sell crypto — sell the product on top of it.

Your cost basis (per transaction)

Cost Approx Notes
Solana network fee ~$0.0003 Negligible.
Gasless sponsorship (Kora) ~$0.001 If you sponsor; optional.
Off-ramp (Sphere) USDC→USD ~0.5–1% Your COGS on the cash-out leg. Pass through + margin.
Infra (Privy, Supabase, RPC) fixed/mo Amortized; favors a SaaS component.

Translation: on the settlement leg your marginal cost is basically zero. Real COGS only appears at off-ramp. That's the pricing seam — bake margin into the fiat conversion where merchants already expect to pay a spread.

Ten merchants like this ≈ $12–20k MRR — and the pitch writes itself: "80% cheaper than their processor, and they get a real product instead of a wallet address." That's a fundable wedge.

Pricing pitfalls to avoid:
  • Don't anchor at 1% like cannabis. Cannabis 1% was positioned against 8% processors as a loss-leader for a market you couldn't reach. Peptides bear more because their pain is worse and they can't easily leave — 1.5% + off-ramp is still a no-brainer for them.
  • Don't make off-ramp free. It's your only real COGS and your best invisible-margin lever. Merchants expect a cash-out spread.
  • Don't skip the SaaS tier. Pure-% revenue is lumpy and unloved by investors; even a small MRR base changes the raise conversation.
  • Don't underprice to win the "vs crypto" headline. They're not really comparing to crypto — they're trapped paying cards. Price to the pain you remove, not the wallet they tolerate.

The gate: legal (do this before anything else)

Many "research peptides" are unapproved drugs sold "not for human consumption" that buyers inject anyway. Facilitating payments can expose you to money-transmitter + aiding-the-sale-of-unapproved-drugs risk. Your non-custodial design and KYB (Sumsub) help but are not a full shield. Get a 30–60 min read from a fintech/payments lawyer before you route a single real dollar. If the answer is "manageable with KYB + clear ToS + non-custodial + no medical claims" → proceed. If it's "you'd be facilitating illegal drug sales" → this is a no-go regardless of demand, and you pivot to a cleaner high-risk vertical (CBD/hemp, supplements) or Path A.
